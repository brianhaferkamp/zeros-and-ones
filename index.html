Universal Books (uBooks)

I've been hearing a lot of talk over the last few years about how eBook sales have gone down steadily from their meteoric rise when they first appeared. As a solution to this problem, I'm presenting uBooks (Universal Books). They are web-based, browser-based alternatives to what we have always known as eBooks.

You might be thinking, "Great. What is a uBook?"

A uBook is essentially a website or web app that holds the content of a single, full book. It has a standard navigation and features chapters or divisions, just like a regular book. The advantages of the open web over proprietary eBook systems that we have now are numerous. Here are some of the best ones I've thought of:

  • Cross-platform
  • Cross-browser
  • Offline capable
  • No reliance on proprietary marketplaces
  • No download necessary
  • SEO-friendly
  • Mobile-first/Responsive
  • Small (Light)
  • Fast
  • Simple codebase for developers
  • No sacrifice in UI/UX
  • Media features are powerful and easy to implement
  • Searchable
  • Compatible with a CMS (e.g., WordPress)
  • Can be printed
  • Capable of being divided in multiple ways (1-page or multi-page)
  • One base form can hold multiple types of content
  • Using one code base, the book can be internationalized (translated) into multiple languages
